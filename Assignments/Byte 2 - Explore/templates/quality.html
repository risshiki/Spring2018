Quality: About This Data Set

This is a dataset that tracks nutritional information across several fast food items. I wanted to compare the average of serving sizes, calories and trans fat content across several fast food brands.

Is the data complete? If not, what is missing and what concerns does it raise?

There were a couple of issues with the data. The serving size and trans fat quantitites for some food portions was not mentioned. Nutritional information varies significantly by serving sizes. If there are any outliers in terms of serving sizes(some brands just serving bigger portions), it would skew the rest of the analysis. Transfat is one of the leading factors correlated with heart disease. Since that was pertinent to my analysis, I decided to also discard rows without it. Ideally, I would have a complete dataset with all macro-nutrient information. However, for the purposes of my analysis, the rest of the data was sufficient.

Is the data coherent?

Yes, the data is coherent.

As one would expect, serving sizes are more or less consistent across brands with the exception of White Castle, which markets itself for it's sliders. Relative to serving size, no significant disparity can be observed amongst most brands with the exception of Subway. A plausible explanation for this may be that Subway markets itself as a brand for the health conscious. Certain brands still have trans fat in trace quantities.

Is the data correct?

Yes. A random sample of 20 rows were verified against MyFitnessPal, another application that tracks nutritional information. No significant disparity was observed.
